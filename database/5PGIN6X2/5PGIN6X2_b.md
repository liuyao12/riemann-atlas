Transformation groups are motivating concrete examples for the abstract notion of a group. A transformation group on an object or space $X$ is a collection of allowed transformations of $X$ closed under identity, composition, and inverse; with composition as the operation, it satisfies the abstract group axioms.

Historically, this is one source of the abstraction: one first studies concrete transformations of a set or space, and later keeps only the formal laws shared by those examples.
