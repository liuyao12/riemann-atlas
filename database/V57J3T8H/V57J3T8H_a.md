A *transformation group* on an object or space $X$ is a collection $G$ of allowed bijective self-maps of $X$ such that

- the identity transformation of $X$ belongs to $G$;
- if $g,h \in G$, then the composite $g \circ h$ belongs to $G$;
- if $g \in G$, then the inverse transformation $g^{-1}$ belongs to $G$.

The word "allowed" is part of the setting. For a bare set, the allowed transformations are permutations. For Euclidean space, the allowed transformations may be isometries. For a topological space, they may be homeomorphisms.
