A *transformation group* on a set $X$ is a collection $G$ of bijective maps $X \to X$ such that

- the identity map of $X$ belongs to $G$;
- if $g,h \in G$, then the composite $g \circ h$ belongs to $G$;
- if $g \in G$, then the inverse map $g^{-1}: X \to X$ belongs to $G$.

The elements of $G$ are transformations of $X$, and the operation is composition. Here "a collection of transformations" means a chosen subcollection of the bijections of $X$, not necessarily the collection of all bijections of $X$.
